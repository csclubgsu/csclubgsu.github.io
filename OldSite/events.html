CS Club presents: "Machine Learning discussion group"

Dear all,

We are glad to present a series of workshops where we will be reading and discussing a book on machine learning and solving practical problems.

BOOK: Kevin Murphy "Machine Learning: A Probabilistic Perspective";

The book assumes basic knowledge of linear algebra, optimization, and probability theory. But! Don't be afraid to stop by even if you feel weak in this areas - you would probably find someone willing to help. If you are interested and do not currently have a copy of the book, we will help as well.

Join us every Friday 12 pm @ 25 Park Placer., conference room.

To be included in this group mailing list, please send me (Olga) a message with your email. Thanks!

All CS Club events are free and open for everyone! You don't have to be a member to attend.

This is a first discussion group of several planned. If you would like to be a leader of any discussion group, please email us with your ideas. To give you an example: interested in bioinformatics? You may create a group which will be reading top scientific papers on particular topic, let say on NGS technologies. We will help you to find groupmates, and will provide food for the meetings! Sounds good? Then email me (Olga) asap!

Email Olga for details and/or to receive Machine Learning email updates: glebova[at]cs.gsu.edu
